# The Treasure of the Hideous One
**by David Cook**
*AC2 — D&D Expert Mini-Adventure for Character Levels 4–7*

---

## Adventure Background

### The Lost Expedition

One hundred years ago, Colonel Rosentos led a troop of 200 soldiers west, ordered by Duke Stefan the Hermit to explore and claim new lands. The colonel sent back periodic reports until his troop reached the small village of Luln — then the reports stopped forever. No more was ever heard from the expedition.

Rumour holds that Rosentos found a fabulous treasure and, preferring not to share it with his men, betrayed them to an evil sorcerer. The rumour has never been proven true.

If the current duke learns of plans to find the treasure, he sends ducal collectors to accompany the party and claim a proper share for his treasury.

### The Treasure

The treasure is located in the cay-men's compound on the Isle of Rosentos. Four possible treasures exist (DM chooses one):

- **Normal treasure:** 10,000 gp; the Jewel of Achmed — a giant sapphire worth 2,000 gp
- **Magical treasure:** The Armory of Morphos — sword +3 (Int 10, Lawful, finds traps, detects magic, extra damage); 20 arrows +1; a stone of controlling earth elementals
- **Combined treasure:** 5,000 gp; 5 pieces of matched jewellery worth 500 gp each or 3,500 gp as a set; a ring of regeneration
- **Special treasure:** The Essence-Orb of Trinkla, the Black Sage — a large semitransparent stone sphere pulsating with living darkness, sitting on a pedestal shaped like a dragon's foot carved from a single bone. The Orb can answer one question per character per month (50% chance absolutely correct, 50% chance absolutely wrong). The power is bound to the stone, pedestal, and building — removing the stone breaks the power; returning it restores it.

## The Veteran's Tale
*(Read aloud to the players as their hook into the adventure)*

*Tharakimeios the Scribe wrote this story as he heard it from the lips of Fondalus the Soldier, and can attest that the words are true. Fondalus said:*

*The soldiers marched from Luln 7 days to the setting sun. On the seventh day, they reached the banks of a great river and camped. That night, a score was lost. One Grisbaldos told the soldiers that spirits worked against the troop, and he told them to go no further. These words were carried to Rosentos and repeated as they were heard. Rosentos held his anger, calling his captains and saying, "These are the words of Grisbaldos, of one who has spoken poorly before." Rosentos then repeated Grisbaldos' words and asked for his captains' counsel. The captains advised Rosentos that Grisbaldos was a danger, for he had spoken against him with evil mischief in his heart. They said, "Grisbaldos is an enemy, and he must be slain." Rosentos spoke against this punishment, but the captains said, "Do not leave this evil wraith at your bosom to destroy us all." So moved by the entreaties of his captains, Rostos agreed.*

*At dawn three of the captains who were loyal to Rosentos delivered Grisbaldos to him and the punishment was given out. Grisbaldos was buried under a cairn of rocks beneath a great oak. All through the day, Grisbaldos' followers wept softly as the troop marched up the river. The way along the river was blocked by swampy shores where the feet of the horses could not make progress. Rosentos called his captains and asked what they should do. Espasios and Melhos said the troops should turn back, but Fondalus advised the making of rafts. Rosentos agreed, and the men built two score rafts to pole through the swamp. The rafts were well-loaded with fruit and water, for the way through the swamp was unknown.*

*For three days, the troop traveled through the swamp. It was here that Hedric the Lame disappeared. Five more were lost to fevers from the bad night air. On the third day, Turim Fellbeard sighted a village on stilts. The villagers greeted the soldiers in long slender boats and led them to their town. Rosentos summoned the village chief to his raft, telling the chief of the great duke. Then Rosentos gave the chief many gifts of glass and ivory. This pleased the chief, who bade a feast for that night. The men ate well, but the seer Kerid Bey said that all should leave. That night, Rosentos had the men sleep with their sandals bound. When a great fog came up, the troop left the village in secrecy. Turim Fellbeard and his raft were lost that night.*

*After much travel, the soldiers sighted a second village. Remembering the dark night before, Rosentos gave orders to pass the village by and not meet with its inhabitants. The troop made for a thick stand of reeds nearby. From this thicket there came a shower of arrows as the men drew close. Many warriors came out of the reeds in their slim boats called ca-noos. They fell upon the raft of Tuchmora and slew many, but Rosentos ordered the rafts together. A great fight was made on this platform, but the bowmen of the troop kept the warriors at bay. The fight began in the morning and continued all day. At last, the warrior chief approached and asked the forgiveness of such mighty soldiers. His tongue was strange, so Kerid Bey told his words. Those of the first village, he said, were demons and enemies of his village. The chief thought the soldiers' rafts were the craft and evil art of the demons.*

*The chief brought gifts of gold and feathers, and he ordered food to be set out for all the men. That night, the men slept dry for the first time in many days. Still Rosentos told his men to keep their sandals laced.*

*That day, the troop lost 7 men. Tuchmora had an arrow in his thigh, and Fondalus had many cuts on his body.*

*For several days, Rosentos stayed at the village. Each night, he spoke at length with the chief. Each day, the chief and his warriors brought more gifts of gold and jewelry. Finally, the captains spoke to Rosentos. "We marvel at the generosity of this chief. Surely you, in your talks with him, have learned the source of his wealth. Share this with us, and we will swear to give you the General's share, as well as make you the duke's treasurer so that you might collect his share." To this Rosentos agreed.*

*The next day, when the chief visited, Rosentos plied him with words and learned from him the source of his wealth. Beyond the edge of the swamp, he said, there lay a great stone house in which treasures might be found. Only the brave could go there, warned he, for the land around it was filled with evil spirits. If Rosentos would drive the spirits away, the chief would show him where to go. Rosentos agreed and called the captains to him. Each man was to prepare himself. Those taken ill by fevers and the night air of the swamp were to wait for the return of the troop. The next day, a troop of two score men left.*

*On the first day, swamp beasts attacked the troop. One beast swallowed a raft, and all on it were lost. Kerid Bey was thrown over the side. The men fired arrows at the beasts, but they scorned these blows and dragged Kerid the seer beneath the waters with them. That day, six men, the captain Melhos, and the seer Kerid Bey were lost. That night, the villagers built a great fire and chanted songs. No man knew the words to these songs, and some claimed they were death songs calling evil spirits.*

*In the morning, Rosentos roused the men, promising them all an extra share. On the second day, the chief led the troop to dry land. The land was the place of the spirits where the treasure lay. Rosentos consulted his captains. Some wanted to go back and wait for the others, but Fondalus and others did not want to face the swamp beasts for no avail. Rosentos decided to leave a small force behind to protect the rafts. Rosentos would lead the rest of the troop inland to find the stone house.*

*For several weeks, the men remained, waiting for word from Rosentos. Perils took the men in the night, until only five remained. Fondalus deemed it wise to return to the village, for now the men there would be healed. Taking one raft, the men set out. On the journey, Fondalus was beset with a fever. Weakened and ill, he could not return, and the men could not find the village without him. Their searchings took them out of the swamp, and so they brought their captain Fondalus back to the lands of the duke. He so survived to tell this tale.*

## Encounter Locations

### 1. Grisbaldos' Grave

Near a slow part of the river is a small rise. A grove of oak trees stands on the top of the rise.

Near the centre of the oak grove, screened from outside view, rests a rotting stump. A scattering of rocks is gathered close to the stump — the remains of a cairn over Grisbaldos' grave.

At night, a pale green light moves and flits through the trees. This is the ghost of Grisbaldos. If the characters camp in the grove, the ghost enters their camp — first as darting glowing balls or a single shaft of light rising from the ground, then slowly forming into the transparent figure of a man in tattered timeworn clothes. His head lolls strangely to one side. Characters may notice thick dark rope burns about his neck.

The ghost approaches with arms outstretched. The moment it touches a character, it evaporates in a cloud of light. If anyone tries to turn the ghost, it breaks apart with a wild maniacal laugh, then a freezing wind thunders through the grove, lifting leaves and small items and flinging them through the air.

**The possession:** Any character touched by the ghost reacts strangely — secretly, they can no longer see, hear, taste, smell, or move of their own will. The possessed character immediately begins asking everyone: *"Where is Rosentos?"* This is all they ask, very insistently, for the next hour.

The ghost of Grisbaldos has temporarily possessed them. While possessed, the character can answer questions about Rosentos' expedition up to the moment Rosentos killed Grisbaldos. Most importantly, Grisbaldos can reveal: how Rosentos' men died in the night (killed by a hydra at the riverbank) and which direction the troop marched along the river (upstream). The possessed character offers no information unless asked.

**The oath:** After 1 hour, the spirit of Grisbaldos departs — but before leaving, it demands a holy oath. The ghost insists the characters find and slay Rosentos, or face its eternal haunting. If they refuse the oath or break it, Grisbaldos' ghost follows them for the rest of their lives, unable to cause physical harm but appearing at dangerous or embarrassing moments. The only way to banish it is to fulfil the oath or visit the most revered member of the possessed character's religion.

### 2. Bandits' Plain

*The 5-foot-tall grass that rises above the steep bank of the river is yellowing under the heat. Dustclouds clog the air. A light breeze blows off the plain and down the river.*

*Suddenly, across the breeze comes the sound of whinnying and the creak of leather. A demonic-looking humanoid rises up from the long grass. His body is swathed from head to toe in thick, black polished armour and metal studs. The creature's face is held tight in a menacing leer. He holds a glittering black blade.*

*Beside the humanoid stands a lean, black horse. Pale fire glows in its eyes. The horse whinnies and stomps as smoke rises from the long grass around its feet.*

*The humanoid speaks in rapid, clicking syllables. It starts forward and says in Common: "Lay down weapons, for you have disturbed me!"*

This is an **elven bandit chieftain** in heavy leathers wearing a demonic mask. His horse is outfitted the same way — phosphorescent paint outlines the horse's eyes, and a small well-contained fire burns near the horse's hooves.

The chieftain is stalling for time. Thirty-five bandits are hidden in the grass, given orders to circle the characters. If the characters do nothing, the bandits surround them in 1 turn.

**Elven Bandit Chieftain:** AC 1; Level 7 Elf; HP 29; carries sword +1 (flames on command), ring of protection +1, ring of fire resistance
Spells: charm person, magic missile, shield / invisibility, web / fireball, hold person / confusion

**Bandits (35):** AC 7; Normal Men; HP 5 each; attack with swords

**Fire hazard:** A flaming weapon has a 10% chance per round of igniting the dry grass. A fire-based spell has 50% chance. Fire grows to a blaze in 5 rounds, then spreads toward the river at 5 feet per round, eventually spreading up and down the riverbank at 15 feet per round.

The chieftain wears a ring of fire resistance — if a blaze starts, he steps into it laughing evilly.

### 3. The Slough of Despair

*The swamp is breezy and cold. The wind ripples the patches of open water between the reeds. Geese take wing, their forlorn honks silencing the few crickets that are out on this bleak day. A pool of stagnant water lies beyond a thick stand of reeds. In the centre of the pool is a hemisphere of darkness. Suddenly, a piercing scream bursts from the heart of the blackness. The reeds explode in a flurry of life as swampbirds shoot up toward the dark sky.*

**If the characters leave:** Nothing happens immediately. After 4 hours, all Lawful characters begin to feel guilty about abandoning the voice. They feel compelled to convince the others to return and help. After 8 hours, Lawful characters insist on returning even if they must leave the party. A *remove curse* spell frees a Lawful character from the compulsion, though they always feel a twinge of regret.

**If the characters enter the darkness:** They find nothing. All characters must make a saving throw vs. magic. Those who fail begin to suffer small nagging doubts about their own abilities — and in combat: they always lose initiative, fight at -1, and save vs. mental attacks (illusions, charms) at -1. This continues until the character helps defeat a monster or NPC of equal or greater level than themselves.

A high-level spell like *commune* reveals this is some type of test set by a god. How well the characters did cannot be learned.

### 4. The Burned Village
*(First village mentioned in The Veteran's Tale)*

*The desolate frames of a few rude huts stand on stilts at the edge of a small, spongy hummock. The huts' roofs are missing, and the floors have splintered and sagged into the water below. The stilts and few scraps of remaining wood are charred. Reeds grow around the huts, clogging the waterways.*

Raided and abandoned long ago, this village is a haven for foul undead. Some floors are still solid; junk is scattered throughout the standing huts.

**Searching the huts:** Every turn the characters search, 10% chance of finding a clue — a crude map carved on a piece of bone. Characters with Intelligence 13+ have a 40% chance to recognise the writing on the bone as related to a language they know. The writing reads: *"Truly the days of Kelshet and his evil hordes are numbered!"* — but there is no indication which village Kelshet led.

**After 1 turn:** 15 ghouls emerge from the water, having climbed the stilts quietly. They attack from all sides. They look as if they have been dead underwater for a very long time.

**Ghouls (15):** AC 6; HD 2; HP 5 each; 3 attacks for 1-3/1-3/1-3 + save vs. paralysis; no treasure.

### 5. The Second Village
*(Second village mentioned in The Veteran's Tale)*

*A collection of six small huts stands in the centre of a small lake of open water. Saplings used as stilts raise the huts above water level. Platforms of woven branches support the crude, reed huts.*

*Furtive movements are noticeable near the huts' doors. A mongrel dog leaps from one platform to another. Several half-rotted canoes tied to the stilts bob slowly in the water.*

When characters are 150 feet away, a shouting voice hails them in an unknown language, then switches to Common: *"You come too soon. Go away. It is not the time."*

If the characters do not reply, 20 arrows fly from the huts.

Immediately after, the natives shout among themselves, then a **gaunt man** steps from the doorway of the largest hut and says: *"Please forgive us, masters. Rooms have been prepared for you. Do not punish us, my masters."* He falls to his knees and kowtows.

**The situation:** This village was overrun long ago by evil natives from the burned village. Since then, they have come under the sway of Rosentos, who uses the village as a food source. The gaunt man is **Chief Korat** — totally under Rosentos' power. He believes the characters are also creatures like Rosentos (they look the same and carry the same equipment). He treats them as masters. If Korat realises the characters are not from the Isle of Rosentos, he has them captured as offerings to Rosentos.

**Korat the Chief:** AC 9; Fighter level 6; HP 23; carries a bone club (mostly ceremonial)

**Natives (35):** AC 9; Normal Men; HP 3 each; more likely to hide in terror than fight

**Two villagers who speak Common:**
- **Carmelita** — granddaughter of one of Rosentos' long-dead men. Very pretty, naive, and charming.
- **Thut** — a tracker who has occasionally travelled outside the swamp. Sullen and suspicious, but loyal and self-sacrificing to those he considers true friends.

If the characters can convince either of them that they are not servants of Rosentos, that NPC agrees to guide the party to the Isle of Rosentos — but they must accompany the party; they cannot simply give directions.

**After 1 week:** Rosentos arrives during the night. If the natives have captured the characters, they are handed over. If not, Rosentos orders the natives to strip the characters of equipment and bring them to the isle on canoes — during which a canoe carrying most of the characters' gear conveniently overturns. The characters arrive just after sundown.

### 6. The Isle of Rosentos

The characters must arrive just before or just after sundown. If this cannot be arranged naturally, have them become lost in the swamp and find the island as the sun sets.

*A small hummock of sandy ground lies ahead. The dark silhouettes of trees are visible in the fading light of day. A small break in the reeds opens up, just wide enough to pass through. A spongy beach lies beyond the reeds, and a trail winds up from the beach into the woods.*

Just after dusk, once the characters have landed, a man comes down the trail from the woods carrying a lantern. He is dressed in crude, obviously homemade clothing but appears in good health — about 35, lean and rangy, with a battered weather-beaten face that makes him rather ugly but not unpleasant. He carries no weapons in his hands, though a sword is strapped to his side.

**This man is Rosentos, the vampire.**

He greets the characters warmly if they are not hostile. He tells them his name is Rosentos (nicknamed "the Hideous One") — the son of the original Colonel Rosentos, who explored and claimed this isle. He claims to have lived here all his life, educated by his father before he died, unable to leave because of the swamp's dangers.

**Rosentos, Vampire:** AC 2; HD 8; HP 40; touch drains 2 levels of experience; can become a dire wolf, giant bat, or gaseous form at will; regenerates 3 HP per round; his gaze charms (characters save at -2); can summon rats, bats, giant rats, and giant bats.

**Clues that something is wrong:** If the characters observe Rosentos closely, his manners, accent, and dress are almost a century out of date.

**The charm attempt:** During conversation, Rosentos secretly attempts to use his gaze to charm each character. Charmed characters overlook all suspicious evidence. Uncharmed characters feel only that Rosentos is "intensely interesting and strangely compelling."

#### 6a. Rosentos' House

*A well-kept wooden cabin stands in a small clearing among tall grasses.*

The cabin has two rooms: a common room and Rosentos' bedroom. Suspicious details: no windows anywhere; no fireplace or cooking arrangement; no food; no mirrors. Rosentos has an explanation for each.

Late in the night, Rosentos retires. He slips out through a secret door, goes to his slave farm to feed, then goes to sleep in a hidden coffin near the slave farm. If the characters left no one to guard their boats, Rosentos steals them that night.

#### 6b. The Slave Farm

*A small collection of dilapidated hovels sits in a swampy hollow. Small gardens grow near the dwellings. A few people mill about the gardens. Others sit outside their homes. All seem to move in a lethargic, dream-like manner.*

Fifteen victims claimed from the village. Rosentos has thoroughly charmed all of them; they cannot act against his will. They speak of Rosentos with great respect.

**Rosentos' Slaves (15):** AC 9; Normal Men; HP 2 each; carry crude clubs when armed.

#### 6c. Rosentos' Coffins

Large stone urns stand by the edge of the path — 5 feet in diameter, topped with heavy stone lids, smooth and featureless. Rosentos uses these as coffins, entering in gaseous form just before dawn. A small amount of grave soil rests in the bottom of each urn.

Four or more characters can tip the urns over using ropes (takes about 1 hour). The first urn investigated is always empty. Rosentos is sleeping in one of the remaining coffins — determine randomly which one.

#### 6d. The Village of the Cay-Men

*The trees break open into a wide clearing, overgrown with thick grass. A space of packed dirt, free of grass, rests near the centre of the clearing. At the very heart of the barren area is a small, unusual compound. Packed dirt and woven reeds make up the walls of this compound. Several large mounds of packed dirt lie beyond the compound walls. Many 1-foot-high openings bore into these mounds.*

*A small group of 8-inch to 12-inch-high creatures stands in the clearing. The creatures look like small lizard men. Some are holding weapons and wearing bone and feather headdresses.*

This is the village of the **cay-men** — small, intelligent, reptilian humanoids related to lizard men but far smaller (no more than 1 foot tall) and more intelligent. They speak Common, their own tongue, and sometimes other local languages. They are not warlike; they fight only for defence or necessary gain.

**The treasure is located here.**

When the cay-men spot the characters, 10 swarm to the compound walls carrying small javelins and bone daggers. They hiss and snap but do not attack if the characters stop or make no threatening moves. If characters attack, the cay-men volley javelins; 5 more cay-men join the wall defence each turn, up to a maximum of 20.

**Cay-men (25):** AC 7; HD 2; HP 9 each; 1 bite or weapon for 1-4 or 1-6; Neutral

If the characters do not attack and simply watch, the **cay-men shaman** eventually appears on the wall. He speaks broken Common, forcing the use of sign language at points. He admits to having the treasure but refuses to give it. He asks pointedly if the characters are friends of Rosentos. If pressed, the cay-men make clear they consider Rosentos a "big-bad man-no-man."

**The deal:** If the characters destroy Rosentos, the shaman agrees to give them the treasure. Otherwise, the characters must wipe out the village (not a Lawful act) or steal the treasure.

**Cay-men Shaman:** AC 6; HD 4; HP 23; when the shaman is present, all cay-men morale increases by 1.

## New Monsters

### Cay-Men
Small, intelligent reptilian humanoids — related to lizard men but no more than 1 foot tall and far more clever. They fashion weapons, build villages, maintain traditions, and speak Common, their own tongue, and sometimes local languages. Not warlike; they fight only for defence or necessary gain. In combat they prefer quick javelin attacks then retreat to cover.

- AC 7; HD 2; Move 90'; 1 bite or weapon; 1-4 or 1-6 damage; Neutral; XP 20

### Giant Serpentweed
A flesh-eating plant found in bogs and standing water. Most of the plant lives underwater rooted to the bog bottom, but sends 1-6 thick scaled stalks above the surface. Each stalk ends in a digestive sac resembling a toothed mouth. From a distance, the stalks look like giant snakes.

The stalks lie just under the water's surface and detect vibrations of passing creatures. They can attack anything within a 20-foot radius. When a stalk loses all hit points it is severed. When all stalks are severed the plant cannot attack, but is not dead — the only way to permanently kill it is to burn the underwater roots. A severed stalk regrows in 6 months.

Immune to fire, illusions, and mind control spells.

- AC 6; HD 9 per stalk; HP 45, 40; Move 0'; 1 bite per stalk; 2-12 damage; Neutral; XP 900 per stalk

## Wandering Encounters (Special)

### Sunken Lair of the Hydra
*(Occurs near the riverbank)*

*The sound of something lumbering and slithering rises ahead. Small branches and reeds snap as the sucking plop of large feet draws slowly closer. Birds squawk and take wing. Suddenly, a loud splash echoes off the river. All is still again.*

The heads of 10 "crocodiles" bob near the shore — actually a single hydra cunningly trying to lure prey to the water's edge.

If 6+ heads strike in a single round, they hold a character and drag them into the river at 5 feet per round. A submerged character can hold their breath for a number of rounds equal to their Constitution score; after that, they save vs. dragon breath or drown.

**Hydra:** AC 5; HD 10; HP 61; 10 attacks for 1-10 each; Neutral

**Lair:** Just downstream — a large hollow in the riverbank, half-filled with water. A mud nest holds 10 hydra eggs. Scattered treasure: 5,000 cp, 2,000 sp, and a solid lapis lazuli bracelet worth 3,000 gp.

### Mudbeasts of the Swamp
*(Occurs in the swamp)*

*The swamp is hot and muggy. Tiny gas bubbles ripple the surface of the water. Suddenly, a loud plop shatters the humid air. Mud splatters everywhere. A sucking sound gurgles from the surface of the swamp as two great serpentine necks shoot up from the muck. The scum-dripping, fanged heads waver 12 feet above the swamp's surface, looking for prey.*

One giant serpentweed with two head-stalks. The plant attacks until slain or until the characters get 20+ feet away — at which point the stalks slither back into the muck. No treasure.

## Summary: The Path to the Treasure

1. Characters receive **The Veteran's Tale** as a clue (treasure map in story form)
2. They travel from Luln westward, following the river
3. **Grisbaldos' Grave** — ghost possession provides key information about Rosentos and the expedition; characters may be bound by oath to slay Rosentos
4. **Bandits' Plain** — ambush by disguised elven bandit chieftain with 35 men
5. **Slough of Despair** — moral test; failure debuffs characters
6. **Burned Village** — ghoul ambush; bone map clue to second village
7. **Second Village** — meet Chief Korat; befriend Carmelita or Thut to get a guide; or be captured and delivered to Rosentos
8. **Isle of Rosentos** — meet the vampire; uncover his true nature; explore the island
9. **Cay-men Village** — negotiate (requires destroying Rosentos first) or steal the treasure
10. **The Treasure** — claim it and escape the island
